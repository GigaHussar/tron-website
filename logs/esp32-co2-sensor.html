Building a CO2 Sensor with ESP32 and ESPHome

February 2026

#hardware#smart_home#automation#network

After setting up Home Assistant I wanted to actually connect something to it. Buying a ready-made sensor felt too easy — I was more curious about building one from scratch. CO2 seemed like the right place to start. You can't see it, you can't smell it, but apparently it has a real effect on how you feel and think. I wanted to know what was actually happening in my room.

I bought an ESP32 and an SCD40 — a sensor that measures CO2, temperature, and humidity. Connected them with short jumper cables, the kind made for small breadboard experiments with Arduino and ESP boards. Then flashed the ESP32 using ESPHome. The whole process was straightforward. No problems getting it into Home Assistant, no failed flashes, no missing libraries. It just worked.

For a while, at least. After some time I noticed the CO2 readings in Home Assistant were frozen — the same value sitting there for hours. That's not possible. CO2 fluctuates constantly depending on what's happening in the room. A quick unplug and replug of the ESP32 would fix it temporarily, but only until the next freeze.

My first assumption was a faulty SCD40. Sensors can arrive dead or fail early. But that would mean ordering a replacement and waiting, and I wasn't ready to accept that without ruling out other causes first.

Then I looked at the cables. They were thin — the type designed for light, low-stress experiments. I swapped them out for thicker ones. The freezing stopped. I haven't seen it happen since.

With stable readings coming in, the next step was doing something useful with them. I wanted a notification when CO2 got high enough that I should open a window. I looked into the options and settled on ntfy — a self-hosted push notification server that runs in Docker. Setup was quick. I pointed Home Assistant at it, then installed F-Droid on my Android phone and grabbed the ntfy app from there. Connected it to my server and created an automation in Home Assistant: CO2 crosses the threshold, notification goes out.

Now when the air gets stale, my phone tells me before I start feeling it.

Monitoring the Air Inside My 3D Printer
#hardware#smart_home#automation#3d_printing

Once the SGP40 and SPS30 were running in my room, a natural next question came up: what's the air like inside the printer itself? Filament heating up, fans running, plastic melting — it seemed worth knowing. And a secondary idea crept in alongside it: what if something went seriously wrong? Overheating plastic, a clog, or worse — a fire. A 3D printer running unattended in a garage feels like the right place for a smoke detector.
